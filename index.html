02 — Sentiment Summary

Cautiously Optimistic Launch Halo, Thin on Organic Reviews

Quickbase Pave launched April 28, 2026, and has generated a wave of broadly positive media and analyst coverage centered on one differentiating claim: it is one of the few AI app-builders that ships governance, data hosting, role-based permissions, SSO, audit trails, and version rollback out of the box, rather than treating those as bolt-on concerns. [The New Stack; Silicon Snark]

Silicon Snark called it "one of the more lucid enterprise AI pitches I have seen lately" and confirmed "the guardrails are real." [Silicon Snark] The New Stack noted that Pave targets vibe coding's notorious "80% problem" — the gap between a working prototype and a production-ready, compliant application. [The New Stack]

Caution flags remain. A 2026 Gartner Peer Insights review of Quickbase AI (predating or concurrent with Pave GA) stated: "Quickbase AI is not even close to ready for primetime; it regularly makes bad assumptions or bad recommendations." [Gartner Peer Insights] Separately, Pave's admin AI controls are currently binary (on/off) with granular per-role restrictions listed as a roadmap item. [The New Stack]

The week of June 15–22, 2026 produced no new external articles, independent reviews, or product announcements about Pave. The only notable event was a scheduled maintenance window on June 18, 2026 (2–3 PM ET) taking down Quickbase US and the Pave US Region. [StatusGator; Quickbase Status] Score carried forward from prior week: 63 / 100 — No Change.

03 — Top Recurring Issues

Issues & Concerns

  • 1
    AI output quality concerns. Gartner reviewer: "Quickbase AI is not even close to ready for primetime — regularly makes bad assumptions." [Gartner]
  • 2
    Binary AI admin controls. No granular per-role or per-department AI access restrictions yet — only all-or-nothing. Listed as a near-term roadmap item. [New Stack]
  • 3
    Opaque credit-based pricing. Free tier has limited build credits; additional credits at $20 increments. Predictability vs. traditional subscriptions remains a concern for budget holders. [Quickbase]
  • 4
    Ecosystem lock-in risk. Pave's full-stack approach keeps users inside the Quickbase data layer. Competitors like Retool offer broader database connectivity. [Digital PM]
  • 5
    Maintenance downtime. Scheduled June 18 outage (2–3 PM ET) affected Pave US Region alongside the main Quickbase platform — no separate resilience tier. [StatusGator]
04 — Top Recurring Feature Requests

Feature Requests

  • 1
    Granular AI access controls. Admins want to restrict AI features at the role, team, or project level — not just a platform-wide toggle. [New Stack]
  • 2
    External data source connectors. Users want to prompt against existing databases (SQL, Salesforce, SharePoint) rather than only Quickbase-hosted tables. [Gartner]
  • 3
    Advanced analytics & dashboards. Request for richer BI-style output beyond the current auto-generated form/table layouts. [Quickbase AMA]
  • 4
    Custom branding / white-label. Enterprise teams want to deploy Pave-built apps under their own brand identity without Quickbase UI chrome. [Quickbase Qrew]
  • 5
    API / webhook export. Requests to expose generated app logic via REST or webhooks so Pave apps can integrate with third-party automation tools (Zapier, Make, etc.). [Quickbase AMA]

📋Sentiment Summary

Quickbase Pave launched on April 28, 2026 to a broadly positive reception across enterprise tech media. Coverage frames Pave as a meaningful answer to "vibe coding's notorious 80% problem" — the gap between AI-generated prototype and production-deployable app. The New Stack, DevOps.com, and Silicon Snark all highlight governance and security as Pave's primary differentiators versus consumer-grade vibe coding tools.

This week (June 8–15): Quickbase published a Leadership AMA recap (June 10) covering CPO Marcus Torres, CRO Josh Allen, CCO Kelly Hall, and CMO Alys Reynders. The Qrew community published a companion post, "From A to Z: Why Pave Feels Like a Big Deal," reflecting grassroots enthusiasm. Two scheduled maintenance windows ran June 10–11. Unanswered AMA questions surfaced continued demand for clarity on roadmap direction and pricing.

The main critical tension: independent analysts want clearer public pricing, more concrete implementation guidance, and a better articulation of where "built in minutes" ends and "maintained competently for years" begins. No negative reviews have appeared on G2/Capterra/Gartner for Pave specifically — the product is still in early adoption.

⚠️Top Recurring Issues

    Pricing opacity — No publicly listed Pave pricing; analysts and prospects repeatedly request transparent tiers before committing.
  • 2
    Long-term maintainability gap — Industry critics ask where the line sits between AI-generated speed and maintainable, evolving enterprise apps.
  • 3
    Enterprise AI security skepticism — OWASP Agentic AI risks (goal hijack, tool misuse, identity/privilege misuse) raised by security-focused coverage.
  • 4
    Platform-wide cost concerns — Existing Quickbase users continue flagging high seat costs ($35–$55/user/mo, 20–40 seat minimums) as a barrier.
  • 5
    AMA unanswered questions — Community ran out of time; users are still awaiting responses on roadmap specifics and Pave–core-Quickbase integration depth.

💡Top Recurring Feature Requests

  • 2

  • 3
    Expanded template/use-case library — Current examples (capacity planning, change request, event mgmt) are appreciated; more verticals requested.
  • 4
    Offline / low-connectivity mode — Recurring ask from field operations and deskless-worker use cases.
  • 5
    Version control / app revision history — Users building governance-critical apps want an audit trail of AI-generated changes.

    Production-ready, not just prototyping — Addresses the "80% problem" by including hosting, deployment, and permissions out of the box.
